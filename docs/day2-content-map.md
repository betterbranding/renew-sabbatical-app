### What are Goals? (Sub-page content)
"Goals are defined as the objects or aims of one's actions. They are the targets we set for ourselves to achieve in different aspects of life, such as personal, professional, or relational. Setting goals helps us to focus our efforts, track our progress, and achieve success.
There are different types of goals, such as short-term and long-term goals. Short-term goals are the ones that can be achieved in a short period, usually within a few days or weeks. On the other hand, long-term goals are the ones that require more time and effort to achieve, usually several months or years."

### What are SMART Goals? (Sub-page content with expandable sections)
- **Specific**: Setting specific goals means defining them with as much detail as possible. Make sure to answer the who, what, where, when, why, and how of the goal.
- **Measurable**: Measurable goals allow you to track progress and determine whether you are on track to meet your goal. Set concrete criteria for measuring progress.
- **Attainable**: Attainable goals are those that are challenging but still achievable. Evaluate whether this is possible given resources and time.
- **Realistic**: Realistic goals are those that are relevant to your overall objectives and feasible given your current situation.
- **Timeline**: Setting a timeline for your goals helps to keep you on track and motivated. Set specific deadlines for each step.
